💡 What is a unit?

A unit is 1% of the bankroll you're willing to invest — so everyone can read these results in their own stake size.
With a $10,000 bankroll, 1 unit = $100. So a system up +157.41u in the first half of 2026 made you $15,741.

That's why POT (betting) ≠ ROI (stocks): treated like stocks, $15,741 / $10,000 = 157.41% in 6 months. The market averages ~10% p.a. (≈5% over 6 months) — about 31× less for the same period. That's the power of turning over and compounding your bankroll.

Median Odds?Median odds are the middle bet's price — half your bets are priced higher, half lower. Unlike a plain average, it isn't dragged up by the occasional longshot multi, so it reflects a typical bet.

Stake-Weighted Odds?Stake-weighted odds average the price by stake, so bigger bets count more. Stake-weighted odds of 2.00 mean that across every unit you risked, the average price was even money (2.00) — a truer read on your book than a plain average, which a few tiny longshots can distort.
